Every year there are many awards ceremonies that honor movies for outstanding writing, directing, acting and much more! This year of course was no different, from the Oscars, to Golden Globes, to Screen Actors Guild Awards many movies were honored this year for truly outstanding work. On this page I'll showcase a couple of awards that some of the movies on the previous page won and talk a little bit more about each one!

Barbie

Award Nominated/Winner
Best Original Song (What was I Made For?) Winner
Actress in a Supporting Role (America Ferrera) Nominee
Costume Design Nominee
Best Picture Nominee
Best Original Screenplay Nominee

Barbie did a phenomenal job with its nominations but didn't end up making as big of a splash in terms of award wins. Many people believe it was robbed of a lot of Oscars, and social media threw a huge fit about Ryan Gosling being nominated for an Oscar for Best Supporting Actor but Margot Robbie not being nominated for Best Actress. This sparked a whole debate in pop culture about whether Margot Robbie was cheated out of an award or not. The less discussed nomination was for America Ferrera for Best Supporting Actress. While she was no Margot Robbie, the community was glad to have some female representation in the awards ceremony. Overall, Barbie was nominated for a ton of awards this season across multiple ceremonies, including the SAG Awards, People's Choice Awards, Critic's Choice Awards, and Golden Globes. Even though it only took home a handful, I'd still consider it a win for Barbie.

Oppenheimer

Award Nominated/Winner
Best Picture Winner
Actor in a Leading Role (Cillian Murphy) Winner
Actor in a Supporting Role (Robert Downey Jr) Winner
Best Original Score Winner
Actress in a Supporting Role (Emily Blunt) Nominee

Oppenheimer had a clean sweep this award season! From Best Picture to Best Original Score, Oppenheimer took home a total of 7 Oscars alone! Not to mention all the awards it won at other award ceremonies. Christopher Nolan is a renound director who has already made a huge splash in pop culture, which partially contributed to the success of Oppenheimer, not to mention the bonus advertising it got from the "Barbenheimer" trend on social media. Along with Oppenheimer, but not mentioned on this page, is Poor Things, starring Emma Stone, which dominated this year's Oscar ceremony. It is also important to note that the awards listed above are just a fraction of the awards Oppenheimer won and was nominated for!

Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse

Award Nominated/Winner
Best Animated Feature Film Nominee
Original Score (BAFTA) Nominee
Best Animated Feature (Critic's Choice) Winner
Best Original Score (Golden Globes) Nominee
Cinematic and Box Office Achievement (Golden Globes) Nominee

Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse did a lot better with its fans than it did with its awards. A truly excellent movie with a fantastic soundtrack was a bit overshadowed by some of the other fantastic movies that came out this year. Similarly to the 1995 Oscars, where Forrest Gump, Pulp Fiction, and The Shawshank Redemption all came out in the same year, All were fantastic movies, but Forrest Gump took a lot of wins that year. This year, there were just so many other good movies that, unfortunately, Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse got overlooked in many areas.

The Super Mario Bros. Movie

Award Nominated/Winner
Best Song (Peaches) (Critic's Choice) Nominee
Best Animated Family Film (Family Film Awards) Nominee
Best Animated Feature (Golden Globes) Nominee
Cinematic and Box Office Achievement (Golden Globes) Nominee

While The Super Mario Bros. Movie did get nominated for some awards in comparison to some of the other popular ones that came out this year, it really didn't do all that well. As mentioned on the previous page of this website, while this movie made a huge impact in the pop culture world and was loved by fans, critics didn't enjoy it quite as much. Because of this, since most awards ceremonies are run by critics, it didn't do quite as well as some of the other movies on this list.

Mission Impossible: Dead Reckoning

Award Nominated/Winner
Best Sound Design Nominee
Best Visual Effects Nominee
Action Movie of the Year Nominee
Cinematic and Box Office Achievement (Golden Globes) Nominee
Outstanding Action Performance by a Stunt Ensemble in a Motion Picture (SAG) Winner

While Mission: Impossible also didn't get as much recognition as many other movies on this page, it's important to note that Mission: Impossible is not generally an "award movie". Mission: Impossible is generally thought of as a stereotypical action movie, which isn't too far off, but Mission: Impossible - Dead Reckoning really pushed the boundaries of action movies with their incredible sound design, visual effects, and stunts!

The Hunger Games: The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes

Award Nominated/Winner
Action Movie of the Year (People's Choice) Winner
Female Movie Star of the Year (Rachel Zegler) (People's Choice) Nominee
Female Movie Star of the Year (Viola Davis) (People's Choice) Nominee
Action Movie Star of the Year (Rachel Zegler) (People's Choice) Winner
Action Movie Star of the Year (Viola Davis) (People's Choice) Nominee

The Hunger Games: The Ballad of Songbirds & Snakes came out towards the tail end of 2023, and because of that, it wasn't recognized in every awards ceremony. The one spot where this movie really killed it was at the People's Choice Awards! This is because it made a huge splash in pop culture due to its nostalgia and marketing strategies mentioned on the previous page. While it may not have been the biggest hit at every award ceremony this year, it made a big enough impact on pop culture to take home a couple People's Choice awards!
